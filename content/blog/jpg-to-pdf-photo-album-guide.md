## Why Convert Images to PDF?

Images are great for viewing, but terrible for formal submissions:

- **Government portals** require PDF for KYC documents, not JPG
- **Job applications** expect a single document, not a folder of photos
- **Email attachments** — one PDF is cleaner than ten separate images
- **Printing** — PDFs maintain consistent sizing across printers; images don't
- **Organization** — a PDF keeps pages in order; a zip of images doesn't guarantee sequence

Converting to PDF gives your images structure, consistency, and professionalism.

## Image Quality Tips

Your PDF is only as good as your input images. A few tips:

**Lighting matters more than camera quality.** A well-lit document photo from a budget phone looks better than a poorly lit shot from an expensive camera.

**Shoot straight down.** Angled shots create perspective distortion. Hold your phone parallel to the document.

**Use a contrasting background.** A white document on a white table is hard to auto-crop. Put it on a dark surface.

**Check before converting.** Zoom in on each image and make sure text is readable. A blurry scan won't magically become sharp as a PDF.

**Crop before uploading.** Remove extra background, desk surface, and fingers from the frame. Clean images make professional PDFs.

## FAQ

**How many images can I convert at once?**
There's no strict limit for personal use. The tool handles 50+ images in a single batch. Larger batches take a few extra seconds.

**Does converting reduce image quality?**
No. Images are embedded in the PDF at their original resolution. The visual quality stays the same.

**Can I control which image goes on which page?**
Yes — drag and drop to rearrange before converting. The order you set is the page order in the PDF.

**What if my PDF is too large after conversion?**
Run it through [Compress PDF](/compress-pdf). High-resolution photos can create large PDFs; medium compression typically reduces size by 50%+ without visible quality loss.

**Can I add text or captions to the images before converting?**
Not in the converter itself. Add text annotations in your phone's photo editor before uploading, or convert to PDF first and then use a PDF annotation tool.
